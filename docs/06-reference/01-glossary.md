# Glossary (plain language)
- Event – A real-world question with a clear outcome (e.g. "Will X happen by date Y?").
- Outcome – A possible answer to the Event (YES, NO, Team A wins, etc.).
- Probability – The current implied chance that an Outcome will happen, based on the money in its pool.
- Event Pool – A pool of money for each Outcome into which all trades flow.
- Leverage – A way to control a larger position while risking a smaller amount of your own cash.
- Cash Portion – The amount of your own money you put into a trade; your maximum loss.
- Stop-loss (Stop) – A probability level at which your position automatically closes to cap your loss.
- Market Impact – How much the displayed probability moves when you place a trade.
- Resolved Event – An Event for which the real-world outcome is known and payouts have been made.
